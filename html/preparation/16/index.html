Back in the day, your computer was mostly a static thing. It stood there under or on your desk and pretty much stayed there. Even laptops don't move all that much but your phone does! Wouldn't it be great if we could do something with all that movement and rotation information? Good thing we can with the DeviceOrientation Event!

What are we talking about?

How does it work?

We add an event listener on the window object which has to listen to the deviceorientation event. Every time the orientation of the device changes the event get's called. Inside the event handler we get the following data:

  • alpha for the rotation around the z-axis (also known as the direction), ranges from 0 to 360 degrees
  • beta for the rotation around the x-axis (also known as the front/back tilt), ranges from -180 to 180 degrees
  • gamma for the rotation around the y-axis (also known as the left/right tilt), ranges from -90 to 90 degrees
  • absolute whether the given degrees are provided as the differences between the Earth's coordinate frame and the device's coordinate frame or the given coordinates are provided relative to an arbitrary device specific coordinate frame.

Useful?

What do you think? Beside it being cool you can do a lot of usefull stuff with this. Gaming comes to mind but there are a lot more applications like:

  • Augmented reality applications where the device orientation is factored in what you look at to show you more relative information
  • Labyrinth style games (you know the one's where you have to direct a marble through a maze)
  • Use your phone as a controller for a game in combination with WebRTC

Great, but can I use it today?

As the deviceorientation matters most on mobile platforms you can actually use this because support for it has been around a while there, the only major exception is the Blackberry browser.

One thing to keep in mind though: the information you get may vary in accuracy per device and even though the browser supports it, your device might not. This is the case for instance with older iPhones that doe not have a gyroscope.
